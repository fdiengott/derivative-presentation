Movement 2 · The staircase

Options: the right, not the obligation.

Below the strike, a floor protects the farmer — just like the forward.

But once the spot climbs past it, their outcome lifts off and rides the market up.

All it ever cost them was the premium.

The farmer pays a premium up front for the right — not the obligation — to sell at the strike. First beat: below the strike they have a floor, just like the forward, sitting a touch lower because of the premium they paid. Then the beat that matters: this season the spot climbs, and the moment it crosses the strike their outcome line lifts off the flat and rides the market up — the cage opens. The faded flat line is where a forward would have trapped them. The only price was the premium.
