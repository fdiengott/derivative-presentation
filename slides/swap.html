Movement 3 · Streams of risk

Swaps: a bumpy stream for a smooth one.

The baker's loan floats — some months gentle, some months frightening.

They swap streams: the baker pays smooth and fixed; the bank takes the bumpy one.

The volatility didn't vanish — it moved to the party built to absorb it.

Two parties only for now. The baker has a variable-rate loan: their payment floats, jagged and unpredictable, and they crave a number they can plan around. The bank's revenue rises with rates, so a floating stream is its natural habitat. So they swap: the baker now pays a smooth, fixed stream to the bank, and the bank sends the floating one back. Watch where the jaggedness goes — it doesn't disappear, it moves to the bank, which is built to carry it. This is the key idea: a swap is risk reshaping, not risk shedding.
