Movement 2 · The staircase

He can never win big. By design.

A hedge isn't a bet you win or lose — it's a bet you've chosen to stop playing.

And it only works if he trusts the other side to show up.

The flat line that felt like safety is also a ceiling: above the strike, all that shaded upside goes to the buyer, not him. That's not a flaw — it's the deal he chose. The real new problem is the last line: a forward is a private handshake. You need someone you trust, and you're frozen into the deal. That launches the future.
