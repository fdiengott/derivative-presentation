Movement 2 · The staircase

He can never win big. By design.

A hedge isn't a bet you win or lose — it's a bet you've chosen to stop playing.

And it only works if he trusts the other side to show up.

The flat line cuts both ways. Below the strike it protected him from a falling price — the downside he avoided. Above it, all that upside goes to the buyer — the price he pays for certainty. Fade both away and you're left with the flat line: he didn't win or lose, he chose to stop playing. The real new problem is the last line — a forward is a private handshake; you need someone you trust, and you're frozen in. That launches the future.
